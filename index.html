How to make a mask yourself?

This pattern was made for sewmask4sf.com.

To make one mask you will need:

  • 1 ⁄ 4 yard of 100% cotton fabric
  • 8” x 16” of filtering material (such as non-woven polypropylene)
  • elastic or bias tape

Instruction

  • Pin right sides of outer and lining layers facing each other. Sew all the way around the edge, except for a 1.5" gap on the bottom edge. Turn right side out and press. Note: Cotton-poly-cotton layered
  • Note: the pointy end of an ironing board is the perfect size and shape to press these masks on. ONLY iron on the cotton side on low heat not on poly to prohibit melting.
  • Top stitch 1/8" around the entire mask, closing up the turning hole as you do.
  • Lay mask with lining side up on table and fold 1" of each end of the mask toward the center. Pin and sew, making the elastic channels.
